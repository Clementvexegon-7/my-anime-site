The Three Fundamental Laws

01

The Law of Equivalent Exchange

The cornerstone of all alchemical practice. To create, one must destroy. To gain, one must sacrifice. The fundamental principle states that the energy and matter involved in an alchemical transmutation must balance perfectly. You cannot create something from nothing, nor can you create something greater than what you sacrifice. This is the price of power, the balance that keeps the universe in order.

Example: To transmute a stone into gold, one must provide equal mass and molecular composition. The alchemist acts merely as the catalyst, rearranging what already exists.

02

The Law of Natural Providence

An object or material cannot be transmuted into something with a different base structure. Lead cannot become diamond. Water cannot become fire. The fundamental nature of matter has its limits, and alchemy must work within those boundaries. One can rearrange, reshape, and reform, but the essential building blocks must remain compatible with the desired result.

Example: You can transmute coal into diamond (both are carbon), but you cannot transmute wood into steel without adding the necessary metallic elements.

03

The Law of Conservation of Mass

Matter cannot be created or destroyed through alchemical transmutation, only transformed. The total mass before the transmutation must equal the total mass after. This law ensures the universe remains balanced. Any attempt to violate this principle results in a rebound‚Äîthe alchemical energy backfires, often with devastating consequences. This is why human transmutation is strictly forbidden.

Example: If you start with 10kg of material, your transmutation result must also weigh 10kg, distributed across the final products.
